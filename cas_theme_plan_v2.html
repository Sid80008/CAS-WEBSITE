The Complete Visual System

What this document covers: Colour, type, spacing, motion, every major page layout, portal UX, component anatomy, image requirements with exact dimensions, and what NOT to do. Covers 27+ public pages and 4 portal tiers.
Chapter 01

Inspiration Verdict

Five reference images analysed. Brutal scoring — what belongs on a school website and what doesn't.

Image 1 — Myna UI (3D Character Hero)
Steal
  • Hero character breaking the frame — subject bleeds below the fold
  • Tight, bold two-line headline. No word waste.
  • Two CTA buttons: one solid primary, one ghost with avatar proof
  • White background that lets the character pop (no dark overlay)
Skip
  • 3D cartoon characters — wrong tone for RBSE-affiliated school
  • The "avatar-as-social-proof" CTA pattern — too startup-y
  • Zero navigation hierarchy — completely flat navbar
What we take: The idea that the hero subject (for us: real students in motion, or a powerful school building shot) should BREAK OUT of its container and breathe into the page.
Image 2 — Channel / The Edit Hatch (Illustrated Scene)
Steal
  • Full-bleed illustrated/painted hero — photo with painterly treatment
  • Mixed type sizes on one screen — massive display + tiny metadata
  • Section transitions that feel like scrolling through a story
  • Content cards that overlap the hero bottom edge
Skip
  • Anime/illustrated characters — not a school context
  • Cluttered navigation with 6 mega-menu dropdowns
  • Sky-blue brand colour — clashes with our navy system
  • The vertical scrolljack sections — kill mobile UX
What we take: "Storytelling scroll" — each section feels like the next chapter, not a generic card dump. Section transitions use diagonal cuts or overlapping elements, not flat borders.
Image 3 — Disney/Pixar Soul UI (Glassmorphism)
Steal
  • Hero character emerges from a dreamy, blurred background
  • Glass cards overlaid on a rich scene — not floating in white void
  • Metadata bar: small details (rating, runtime) make it feel alive
  • Soft gradient that fades toward white at the bottom — elegant
Skip
  • Full glassmorphism everywhere — over-used, not school-appropriate
  • Purple-lavender palette — wrong for CAS brand
  • Movie-UI reading pattern — entertainment, not information
What we take: The "glass panel on rich background" treatment for our hero section. Hero image fills full width, school name + tagline sits in a frosted-glass pill anchored to the bottom-left. Feels premium, not SaaS.
Image 4 — Pixar Animation Studios
Steal
  • Hero split: large scene left, character card panel right
  • Content catalog with date/release filters — straight to facilities page
  • Typography contrast: serif display headline + sans body in same block
  • Color-block section breaks — navy, then white, then photo-background
  • Gallery grid that feels editorial, not widget
Skip
  • Movie poster grid — wrong content type for school
  • Dark-on-dark text sections — accessibility fails
  • Horizontal scrolling filmstrip — frustrating on mobile
What we take: The "catalog with depth" layout for our Academic Achievements / Gallery pages. Large featured item (Topper of the Year) left. Grid of all toppers right. Feels like editorial journalism, not a school website table.
Image 5 — Kamui (Japanese Scene, Illustrated)
Steal
  • Scene has depth: foreground / midground / background layers
  • Atmospheric lighting — golden hour warmth in the sky
  • The calm, "institutional peace" feeling — trust and tradition
  • Lanterns as accent details — we adapt with warm amber glow points
Skip
  • Fantasy illustration style — not a school aesthetic
  • The mysterious darkness — school needs to feel safe and open
  • Japanese architecture — obviously irrelevant
What we take: "Layered depth" in our hero photograph. We achieve it with parallax: campus building in back, students in middle, text in front. The amber/warm-gold sunset feeling translates directly to our amber accent system.

Chapter 03

Motion & Animation

Every transition has a purpose. Nothing moves without a reason. Speed communicates confidence.

Entrance — Hero
duration: 1.2s
ease: [0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]
Fade up + scale from 1.05. The zoom-out says "this place is bigger than you expected."
Scroll Reveal
y: 32px → 0
opacity: 0 → 1
delay: stagger 0.08s
Cards reveal one by one as you scroll. Not all at once — that's lazy.
Button Hover
translateY: -2px
boxShadow: +4px
duration: 220ms
Lift effect on hover. Confirms interactivity without distracting.
Page Transition
opacity: 0 → 1
duration: 300ms
ease: easeOut
Simple fade. No slides, no flips. School parents aren't a gaming audience.
Card Hover
translateY: -4px
shadow: medium → strong
duration: 200ms
Lifts off the surface. Combined with border darkening on hover.
Counter Animation
0 → target
duration: 1.8s
ease: easeOut
trigger: viewport
Stats on homepage count up when you scroll into view. Emotional impact.
Image Parallax
Hero bg: 0.15x scroll rate
Lazy on mobile
Hero photo moves slower than the page on desktop. Creates depth.
Notice Ticker
marquee: 40s linear
pause on hover
direction: RTL
Scrolling notice strip below hero. RTL because Rajasthan parents often read Hindi first.
Gallery Hover
scale: 1.04
overlay: opacity 0→0.2
duration: 350ms
Photos breathe on hover. Blue overlay tint maintains brand in gallery view.
Mobile rule: prefers-reduced-motion must disable all transforms and parallax. Counter animation becomes instant. Page transitions remain (they're functional).

Chapter 07

Design Principles

The difference between a premium website and a template. Seven rules that every component, every page, every line of code must obey.

01 — Depth Over Flatness
  • Hero text always overlays the photo. Never below it.
  • Cards overlap section borders — never floating in isolation
  • 3 layers minimum in every hero: background photo / overlay / text + CTA
  • Quick-access cards start inside the hero and extend below the stat bar
02 — Asymmetry Is Intelligence
  • About pages: image on one side, text on other — NEVER centered
  • Section headers default left-aligned, center only for isolated CTA sections
  • One "editorial break" per page: a full-bleed photo between text sections
  • Toppers page: feature #1 large, #2 and #3 stacked — not 3 equal columns
03 — One Signature, Many Expressions
  • The amber line appears on: stat bar, section headers (48px×4px), featured card borders, topper rank badges, CTA button colour
  • It never fills a large area. It is always a line or a highlight.
  • If a parent visits 5 different pages, amber should appear in 5 consistent ways
04 — Real Over Polished
  • Use real school photos even if slightly imperfect — AI-looking stock photos immediately destroy trust with Rajasthan parents
  • Staff cards with real initials are better than "image loading" placeholders
  • Real topper names and percentages in the actual hero — not generic "Award Winner" text
  • Director's message must have a real signature scan, not typed name
05 — Hindi Is First Class
  • Every notice must have Hindi subtitle at equal visual weight
  • Language toggle in nav must be prominent — not tucked away
  • CTA buttons on homepage appear in both: "Apply Now / अभी आवेदन करें"
  • No font-switching flash: use Noto Sans as fallback for all Devanagari
06 — Mobile Is Primary
  • All touch targets minimum 44×44px
  • Bottom navigation bar on mobile portals (not sidebar)
  • Fee receipts and notices must be full-readable on 375px screens
  • Hero height: 100svh on mobile, 620px fixed on desktop
07 — Speed Is Respect
  • All hero images: WebP format, next/image with priority
  • Portal dashboard must load in <2s on 4G (Anta network speeds)
  • Never load more than 6 images above the fold
  • Fonts: preload only Plus Jakarta Sans 700+800, Inter 400+600
What This Site Is NOT
✕ Not a Government Portal
No full-width grey banners, no table-first layouts, no "Visit Counter" widgets, no blinking text, no Comic Sans adjacent fonts.
✕ Not a SaaS Landing Page
No pure white floating cards on white background. No "free trial" hero hooks. No mesh gradients or neon glows. No testimonials with generic stock faces.
✕ Not a WordPress Theme
No full-width colored banner every 200px. No widget-box syndrome. No 4 different font sizes per page. No "latest tweets" sidebar. No generic countdown timers.
✕ Not an AI-Generated Template
No glassmorphism everywhere. No particle animations. No hero with "smiling diverse students" stock image. No generic "Our Mission, Vision, Values" in three equal cards.

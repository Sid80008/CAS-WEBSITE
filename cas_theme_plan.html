What to steal from that image

Take these ideas

  • Circular image cards for staff
  • Hero scene with text layered on image, not below it
  • Quote/testimonial section with person visible
  • Category pills as section headers
  • Content bleeds across section breaks

Leave behind

  • Synopsis text dumped in a floating box
  • No breathing room between elements

  • All-caps everywhere (tiring to read)
  • Decorative elements with no function

Why this combination: Navy = authority, trust (parents expect this from a school). Gold = achievement, excellence (works for toppers, awards, important notices). Warm white instead of pure white = more human, less clinical. The green avoids the site looking like a government portal.

Design principles — what makes it look real, not AI-generated

Overlap things

Hero text sits on the image, not below it. Cards slightly overlap section dividers. Nothing lives in its own isolated box.

Asymmetric sections

Not every section is full-width centered. About page: image left, text right. Principal message: quote overlapping photo.

One signature shape

The gold diagonal stripe (like the anime's color blocks). Use it as a section break, not a flat horizontal rule.

Real photo textures

Background sections use actual school building/grounds photos at 15% opacity — not flat colors or stock gradients.

Mixed language rhythm

Hindi text isn't hidden or secondary. It appears alongside English at the same visual weight — notices, labels, taglines.

Consistent corner radius

12px on cards, 8px on chips/tags, 0px on full-bleed sections. The anime image mixes these randomly — that's what looks cheap.

What this theme is NOT
Not a government portal — no full-width gray headers, no Comic Sans-adjacent fonts, no table-based layouts
Not a corporate SaaS site — no pure white backgrounds with floating cards on white, no generic hero with stock photo of smiling students
Not an AI-generated template — no gradient mesh backgrounds, no glass-morphism, no neon accents, no "hover for floating card" tricks
Not a cheap WordPress theme — no full-width colored banners every 200px, no widget-box syndrome, no 4 different font sizes on one page
